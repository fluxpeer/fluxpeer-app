or later (**AGPL-3.0-or-later**), as stated in [`LICENSE`](LICENSE) and
[`LICENSE-AGPL-3.0.txt`](LICENSE-AGPL-3.0.txt).

As an **additional permission under section 7 of the AGPL**, the copyright
holders of `fluxpeer-app` grant the following exception.

## Grant

You are permitted to convey the Program, and to distribute applications built
from it, through Apple's App Store, the Google Play Store, or any comparable
application marketplace, **even though that marketplace's Terms of Service impose
usage rules, copy or device limitations, or digital rights management (DRM) that
would otherwise be additional restrictions prohibited by section 7 (and the
downstream-licensing rule of section 10) of the AGPL.**

To the extent such marketplace terms apply to a copy obtained from that
marketplace, you and every recipient of that copy are granted the additional
permission necessary to comply with those terms while remaining in compliance
with the AGPL. This resolves the well-known conflict between (A)GPL section 7 and
app-store terms of service (the "VLC problem").

## Scope and limits

- This permission applies **only** to distribution through such marketplaces. It
  does not waive any other right or obligation under the AGPL — in particular, the
  obligation to make the **Corresponding Source** of the conveyed version available
  to recipients still applies.
- A recipient who obtains the source **independently** of a marketplace continues
  to receive it under the plain terms of the AGPL, without these marketplace
  restrictions.
- Anyone who conveys a **modified** version of `fluxpeer-app` may keep or remove
  this additional permission for their modified version, at their option, as
  provided by AGPL section 7, paragraph 4.

## Note

This exception is what allows the fluxpeer project (the copyright holders) — and
anyone else — to ship builds of this AGPL application through Apple's App Store
and Google Play without the store's terms of service conflicting with the AGPL.
